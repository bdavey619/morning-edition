# Contributing to Morning Edition

Morning Edition is a small, editorially driven project. Contributions are human-first: AI assists, but every published piece of content has a human responsible for it.

This document is for people who want to suggest stories, contribute as editors, or help verify local logistics.

---

## How to suggest a story

Open an issue or send a message to the editor with:

1. **What happened** — a factual one-sentence summary
2. **Why it matters locally** — why would a San Diego reader want to know this?
3. **A source link** — a working URL from a credible outlet or official source
4. **Which section it fits** — Front Page, Around Town, Looking Ahead, Storyline, Worth Your Time

If you're not sure which section, just describe it and the editor will decide.

---

## How to suggest an event

For Around Town or Looking Ahead, include:

- Event name
- Date and time (exact, not approximate)
- Location / neighborhood
- Cost (free or specific amount)
- Source URL (event website, official organizer page, or verified local media)
- Why you think it's worth leaving your neighborhood for

If you don't have a source URL for the date, time, location, or cost — don't submit it yet. Find the source first.

---

## How to verify local logistics

Logistics verification is the most important contribution a local reader can make.

Before any practical detail goes into an edition, someone needs to confirm:

- **Parking:** Is the area actually hard to park in? Describe what you know from personal experience. Note if street parking is residential-only, metered, or genuinely available.
- **Transit:** Does a trolley or bus actually go there? What's the route number? Does it run on weekends? Confirm at [sdmts.com](https://www.sdmts.com).
- **Cost:** Is the event actually free? Is there a suggested donation? Is there a ticket required? Link to the official page.
- **Hours/dates:** Is the time correct? Events often change. Link to the source.
- **Location:** Is the address right? Is it the main entrance?

If you've personally been to a venue or event, that local knowledge is valuable — but label it as such (editor knowledge, not a confirmed source).

---

## What should never be published without verification

These details must be verified before they appear in any logistics section:

- Transit routes and stop names (trolley or bus)
- Bus route numbers
- Parking availability or cost
- Event dates and times
- Ticket prices or admission costs
- Venue addresses

If you cannot find a reliable source for any of these, the detail should be removed or replaced with "verify before you go."

**Ocean Beach specifically:** No San Diego Trolley service exists in Ocean Beach. Do not suggest trolley routes to OB. Direct readers to [sdmts.com](https://www.sdmts.com) to look up bus routes.

---

## Tone guidelines

Morning Edition sounds like a trusted local friend who reads a lot and doesn't have an agenda. When writing or suggesting content:

- Be specific, not vague
- Be warm, not promotional
- Be factual, not opinionated
- Be local, not parochial
- Avoid clickbait framing
- Avoid partisan language
- Avoid manufactured urgency

If a headline sounds like it belongs on a news aggregator designed to make you angry, it's probably not right for Morning Edition.

---

## How AI should and should not be used

**AI can:**
- Draft initial summaries and section copy
- Suggest candidates based on a topic
- Format structured content (JSON)
- Generate trivia questions (with editor verification)
- Write hallway questions

**AI cannot:**
- Verify facts
- Confirm transit routes or event logistics
- Set `provenance` to `human_reviewed` — only humans do that
- Publish content
- Replace the editor's judgment

When in doubt: if AI wrote it and you haven't checked it, it's `ai_drafted`. Don't publish `ai_drafted` content.

---

## The editor's responsibility

The human editor is responsible for everything that appears in a published edition. This is not a burden — it's the point. Morning Edition is editorially legible because someone put their name on it.

Before marking anything `human_reviewed`, the editor should be able to say:
- "I read this."
- "The facts check out against the sources linked."
- "The logistics are accurate to the best of my knowledge."
- "I would personally share this."
