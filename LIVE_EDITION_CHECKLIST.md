# Live Edition Checklist

Run this before publishing any real edition — one where actual people will read it.

This is not the same as the pre-publication fact-check in `prompts/fact-check-checklist.md`. That checklist covers individual section accuracy. This one covers whether the edition as a whole is ready to share with real readers.

---

## Content

- [ ] Is every factual claim in the Front Page traceable to a source link?
- [ ] Is the Front Page headline accurate — not misleading, not overstated?
- [ ] Are event dates and times verified against the official event page or organizer?
- [ ] Are location and neighborhood labels accurate?
- [ ] Is the cost verified? (Free means actually free. Not "usually free.")
- [ ] Is the Worth Your Time article real, by a real author, at a real publication?

## Logistics

- [ ] Are parking notes based on real knowledge of the area — not AI inference?
- [ ] Are transit notes verified against official MTS sources (sdmts.com)?
- [ ] If Ocean Beach is mentioned: does the transit note correctly say no trolley serves OB?
- [ ] Are any rideshare notes honest about expected wait times or surge conditions?
- [ ] Are there any `ai_inferred` logistics still marked as such in the JSON? If so, verify or remove them before publishing.
- [ ] Is there anything in an If You Go or Getting There section that you could not personally stand behind?

## Editorial

- [ ] Did a human read the complete edition before setting `published: true`?
- [ ] Does every section have an appropriate `provenance` value? (No `ai_drafted` in published content.)
- [ ] Does the edition include at least one conversation starter?
- [ ] Does the edition include at least one thing that gives a reader a reason to leave their neighborhood or participate locally?
- [ ] Is the Hallway Question genuinely easy to answer and low-stakes?
- [ ] Does the Mini Puzzle answer hold up? (You looked it up, not just trusted the AI.)

## The feel test

- [ ] Would you personally send this edition to 5 people you respect?
- [ ] Does the edition feel finished — like it has an end?
- [ ] Is there anything in it that made you wince when you re-read it?
- [ ] Does it feel like morning? (Calm, useful, not alarming.)

---

## If you answered no to anything above

Fix it, cut it, or wait until tomorrow.

A delayed edition is better than a wrong edition. There is no deadline pressure here. The trust you're building with readers takes months. The trust you can lose with one bad logistics note takes one bad experience.
