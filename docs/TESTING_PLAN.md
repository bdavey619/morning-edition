# Morning Edition — Early Reader Testing Plan

**Goal:** Observe behavior. Not collect a wishlist.

The purpose of this first round of testing is to find out whether Morning Edition is doing the thing it's supposed to do: helping people notice more, have better conversations, and participate in San Diego.

Send one live edition to 5–10 people. Ask three questions. That's it.

---

## Who to send it to

People who:
- Live in San Diego (or a specific neighborhood)
- Read the news in some form already
- Are willing to give a brief, honest response
- Are not primarily developers or product people (they'll give feedback on the wrong things)

You want readers, not reviewers.

---

## What to send

A direct link to the live edition. No preamble, no explanation of what it is or how it works. Let the product explain itself.

If it needs a preamble to make sense, that's a product problem to fix before the next round.

---

## What to ask

After they've had a chance to read it:

**1. What did you talk about?**

Did anything from the edition come up in conversation — with a partner, a coworker, a friend? What was it?

**2. Did you consider going somewhere?**

Did the Around Town or Looking Ahead section make you consider doing something, going somewhere, or planning anything? Did you end up going?

**3. Would you open this again tomorrow?**

Yes / no / maybe, and a brief reason if they have one.

---

## What not to ask

Do not ask:
- "What features would you want to see?"
- "What was missing?"
- "How would you rate it?"
- "Would you pay for this?"
- "What would make you share it?"

These questions produce wishlists, not insight. You're not building features yet. You're finding out if the core product works.

---

## What you're listening for

**Green signals:**
- "I mentioned [X] to someone"
- "We talked about [the hallway question / the game / the housing thing]"
- "I looked up [the event / the restaurant / the trolley]"
- "I actually went"
- "Yes, I'd open it tomorrow"

**Yellow signals:**
- "I skimmed it"
- "I read part of it"
- "It was interesting but I forgot about it"
- "I'd read it if it came to me somehow" (passive engagement)

**Red signals:**
- "I didn't finish it"
- "I wasn't sure what it was for"
- "Nothing stood out"
- "No, I don't think I'd open it again"

---

## After the first round

Collect the responses. Look for patterns, not outliers. One strong reaction (positive or negative) is data but not a decision.

If most people talked about something from the edition → the shared context is working.
If most people considered going somewhere → the participation layer is working.
If most people would open it again → the product has pull.

Fix one thing, then test again.

---

## What this testing plan is not

This is not a user research study. It is not statistically significant. It is not a product roadmap exercise.

It is a way to find out, cheaply and quickly, whether Morning Edition is doing the thing it was built to do.
